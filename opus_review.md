# Result
This is a strong, unusually honest paper — I'll give you the adversarial read you asked for. I have the full PDF in context, so this is a close review rather than a skim. Summary first, then recommendation, then concerns ordered by severity.

## Summary as I understand it

A continuous-time RIO system on a quadrotor: quintic position B-spline + cumulative SO(3) orientation B-spline, fixed-lag smoother (3 s window, 0.3 s stride) with Schur marginalization, single-chip TI IWR6843AOP + 1 kHz IMU, evaluated on racing and 10 rad/s backflips. Three headline contributions: (1) a "Markov-blanket" factor-selection rule that makes the marginal exact and kills prior-scale tuning; (2) one dynamics-adaptive weighting law covering hover→10 rad/s; (3) characterized sensor systematics, notably that the tumbling elevation bias is a flight-regime proxy, not an antenna constant. Plus bidirectional cross-validation against the Doer/Trommer EKF-RIO family and a set of documented negative results.

## Recommendation

**Major revision.** The engineering is real, the negative-results posture is exactly what T-RO should reward, and reproducibility is exemplary. But three things in the current framing are either overclaimed or internally contradictory, and the statistical basis is thin for a journal. None are fatal; all are fixable without new experiments except possibly (5).

## Major concerns

**1. The marginalization contribution is framed as more novel than it is.** Correct Schur-complement marginalization (OKVIS, VINS-Mono) already includes *only* factors connected to the marginalized states and does not condition on interior states or double-count. What you describe as the "naïve" baseline — pulling in every IMU factor through the shared bias, conditioning on interiors, double-counting — is an implementation bug, not a property of standard marginalization. So "make the Schur marginal exactly closed" reads, to a reviewer who has implemented this, as "we did textbook marginalization correctly." The genuinely novel and useful part is narrower and you should foreground it: in a continuous-time B-spline window with a *global shared bias*, the bias block tempts you to include far-edge IMU factors, and the fix is to gate on "touches a *marginalized* variable" specifically rather than "touches marginalized *or* boundary." That shared-bias trap is the real insight. You cite Hug et al. [15] as already applying Schur marginalization to VI splines — you must state concretely what [15]'s (or standard) factor selection does and why it fails here, or the delta against prior art looks negligible. The diagnostic symptom-pattern (Section VII-C) and the re-centering-instead-of-FEJ choice are independently valuable; lead with those. (Aside, as a positive: your boundary dimension 30 = 5 position CPs×3 + 3 orientation knots×3 + 6 bias is internally consistent with quintic N=6 and cubic N=4 local support — but you never state N explicitly, so the reader can't verify the containment argument [i, i+N−1] ⊆ marg∪boundary. State it.)

**2. The "sensor-only initialization" claim is contradicted by your own P1.** Section IV-D: "MoCap orientation is used for a full 3-D accelerometer bias estimate when available; otherwise a gravity-aligned scalar correction is applied." That is an external pose reference entering initialization, which directly undercuts "P1–P3 and the position/attitude estimate use no external pose reference" and the abstract's framing. You must state unambiguously which configuration produced the headline Table II numbers. If MoCap-aided accel bias was used, the "sensor-only" claim has to be qualified everywhere it appears, or you need to show the headline numbers under the scalar-correction (truly sensor-only) path. This is the single biggest honesty/reproducibility exposure in the paper.

**3. The adaptive-knot-density "disproof" is claimed but never shown, and it contradicts Section VII-B.** Contributions and Conclusion both assert you *disproved* the adaptive-knot-density hypothesis ("the orientation spline already tracks 10 rad/s flips at the gyroscope noise floor"). But there is no section, table, or figure presenting that study anywhere in the body — and Section VII-B "Remaining gap" proposes "adaptive (non-uniform) knot placement and per-chirp timestamps" as the *future-work lever* for the residual flip error. As written these read as a flat contradiction. I suspect the resolution is that uniform knots already saturate *orientation* tracking (so denser knots don't help attitude), while the remaining *position/loop-scale* gap is intra-frame Doppler smearing, where per-chirp timestamps — not knot density per se — are the lever. If so, say exactly that, separate the two axes explicitly, and *add the representation-level study to the body*. A claimed negative-result contribution with no supporting evidence will be challenged on sight.

**4. The NEES consistency claim is under-powered and the statistic-switching looks like cherry-picking.** You compute NEES against MoCap over windows of a *single* trajectory per condition. Consecutive windows are highly correlated, so the χ²₃ mean-3 expectation doesn't strictly apply without an effective-sample-size argument or multiple runs. More visibly: you report orientation NEES as a *mean* (0.7–0.9×, flattering) but velocity NEES as a *median* (1.4–5.7) and attribute the velocity *mean* to unreliable MoCap reference — switching the summary statistic per channel and explaining away the inconvenient one is exactly what a reviewer flags. On backflips only 6/50 windows survive the quality mask, which is far too few to support any consistency statement. Either (a) report the same statistic for both channels with the distribution shown, (b) caveat the temporal-correlation limitation explicitly, and (c) soften the abstract from "NEES-consistent orientation covariance" to "approximately calibrated on racing flights." As stated, the abstract claim outruns the evidence.

**5. Statistical thinness: one representative recording per condition.** You acknowledge this, and the held-out flights + bit-identical re-runs help — but re-runs add no *statistical* power (same data), and N=1 per regime with no error bars is below what T-RO typically expects. This is the concern most likely to actually require new data. At minimum, the limitation needs to be stated more prominently than a clause in Section VI-E; ideally you add 2–3 more recordings per regime even if only in supplementary material.

## Moderate concerns

**6. "Position parity" overstates Table III.** ekf-yrio beats "Ours" on absolute position in all four ICINS flights (0.39<0.92, 0.20<0.51, 0.47<1.92, 0.22<0.92), and on flight 3 by 4× in both meters and drift%. "Same order of magnitude" is defensible; "parity" (Conclusion) is not. Your real win there is the vertical-bias robustness via the RANSAC prefilter and the heading-aided orientation — say that, and stop claiming you match them on position when you consistently trail the yaw-aided baseline.

**7. The real-time claim is conditional and should be stated as such.** Per-window solve is 0.35–0.70 s against a 0.3 s stride, so the system is *not* real-time at the nominal stride in any regime; "real time for aggressive racing" only holds after relaxing the stride to 0.4 s (and even 0.35 s > 0.3 s). The abstract should qualify this rather than assert real-time.

**8. The settled backflip velocity (3.47 m/s, starred as a "resampling artifact") undermines the settled-evaluation methodology, not just that cell.** If stride-boundary resampling can inject a >3 m/s artifact into one channel, a reviewer will distrust the settled column generally. Either fix the resampling so settled is reportable, or drop settled for backflips entirely and say why.

**9. The "magnetometer surrogate" is noise-free MoCap yaw, which optimistically biases heading-aided orientation.** A real magnetometer has bias, disturbance, and noise that MoCap doesn't. Your roll/pitch decomposition (Table III(b)) and unaided-yaw check are the right mitigations and you should lean on them — but the headline orientation numbers are heading-aided with a perfect reference, and the abstract's "<3°" should carry that caveat.

**10. Reproducibility is excellent but the per-regime hyperparameters are scattered.** ∆tp ∈ {5,40,10} ms, ∆to = 8 ms (window) vs 0.8 ms (backflip batch), λ values, anchor settings, b=−1.5, heading-weight overrides — these are spread across §IV, Table II footnote, and §VII-B. At least four different "slow racing batch position" numbers appear (0.157/0.169/0.183/0.198/0.20/0.314) reconciled only by footnotes. Add one consolidated per-regime configuration table; it'll also defuse the "which number is the real one" reviewer reflex.

**11. No compute comparison against the EKF baselines, and the reverse port's scientific value is limited.** The EKF baselines run near sensor rate; you run 0.35–0.70 s/window. The accuracy/consistency-vs-compute trade is never tabulated. Separately, porting someone's filter to an out-of-distribution platform and showing it rejects 99.7% of updates mostly shows it wasn't designed for your stack — which is unsurprising. You already frame it as "measured outcome, not a verdict," which is the right posture; consider explicitly labeling the reverse port a *sensor/stack-coupling characterization* rather than a comparison.

**12. Timing inconsistency:** Section IV-E says the marginalization step costs ~10 ms/window, but Section V says "the linear solve and the marginalization-prior recompute (compute_prior) are the dominant costs." 10 ms out of 350–700 ms is ~2%, not dominant. Reconcile — either compute_prior ≠ the 10 ms step, or "dominant" is wrong.

## Minor / consistency / citations

- **§II: "Doer et al. [1]"** — [1] has two authors; use "Doer and Trommer." Same for any other two-author "et al." instances.
- **Undefined-on-first-use acronyms/tools:** *reve*, *RVE* (§II, Kramer), *basalt*, *x-rio*, *ekf-yrio* — define each at first mention and fix typography (these flip between roman, lowercase, and what looks like intended typewriter/small-caps).
- **[15] differentiation** (see Major 1) is also a citation-completeness issue: it's your closest methodological neighbor and gets one sentence.
- **Metric-ordering inconsistency:** the abstract gives tumbling as "2.9%/6.3°" (drift/ori) but elsewhere you order velocity/orientation/position. Pick one order.
- **Title:** "Sliding Window" → "Sliding-Window" (adjectival). And "6-DOF state estimation" in the title is undercut by "absolute position is not observable from Doppler+IMU" — defensible since you do estimate a full trajectory, but consider whether the title oversells observability.
- **Doppler alias unwrapping** is described only in P2/init. Is unwrapping carried into the main solver's Doppler factors, or only used to seed? If aliased points reach the solver unwrapped, Huber caps but doesn't correct them — clarify.
- **Fig. 4** shows only slow/fast racing error timeseries; a backflip error-vs-time panel would materially strengthen the tumbling claims (Fig. 3 only gives the overlay).
- **Table I** backflip vmax/ωmax are 95th-percentile with "MoCap artifacts dominate peaks," which means the "10 rad/s" headline is itself measurement-limited — state whether 10 is the absolute or typical peak.
- **Table II layout** is hard to parse (the twin/Drift% columns run together); reflow for camera-ready.
- **arXiv/venue years:** [8] Ng et al. is IROS 2021 but arXiv:2201 (Jan 2022) — check ordering; verify all bibliographic details against the actual sources, which I haven't independently fetched.
- **Abstract** is very long and crams every result; tightening it will improve first impressions with editors and reviewers.

## What's genuinely strong (keep it)

The research-question framing (can it work → what it takes → where it fails) is excellent and rare. The negative results — the prior-scale symptom pattern as a reusable diagnostic, the b-is-a-flight-regime-proxy disproof of the antenna-bias reading, the GNC-masks-calibration-errors finding, the full-extrinsic "trash can" result — are the most valuable part of the paper and exactly what the field underreports. Released code, seeded RANSAC, deterministic re-runs, and full hyperparameter disclosure are model behavior. The dynamics-adaptive weighting with the (ωr,ωa)=(4,8) Pareto-knee justification is a clean, defensible piece of engineering.

If you fix the three contradictions (sensor-only, knot-density, marginalization framing) and temper the four overclaims (parity, real-time, NEES, abstract orientation), this is a publishable T-RO paper whose honesty is its differentiator.
