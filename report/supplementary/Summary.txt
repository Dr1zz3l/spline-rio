Supplementary Video — Summary
Continuous-Time Radar-Inertial Odometry with B-Spline Sliding Window Optimization
T. Weiss and L. Pries (Technical University of Munich)

The video shows the proposed continuous-time radar-inertial odometry system running on
the three flight regimes evaluated in the paper — gentle racing, aggressive racing, and
attitude-tumbling backflips (up to 10 rad/s). For each flight it overlays the live
sliding-window estimate (the leading-edge trajectory a deployed system would emit) against
the Vicon motion-capture ground truth, alongside the radar point cloud. The backflip
sequence illustrates the regime in which the single-chip radar measurement model degrades
(intra-frame Doppler smearing) and the dynamics-adaptive measurement weighting takes over,
which is difficult to convey in static figures. No audio. Approximately <DURATION>.
